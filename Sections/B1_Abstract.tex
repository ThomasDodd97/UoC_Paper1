\begin{center}
    \begin{large}
        \textbf{Abstract}
    \end{large}
    \begin{justify}
        \begin{normalsize}
            Glass fibre-reinforced polymer composites are widely used in the
             wind turbine manufacturing industry, and are commonly manufactured 
             using general-purpose unsaturated polyester resin thermosets.
            However, these resins are derived from oil-derived aromatic
             monomers making them both unsustainably sourced and hard to
             recycle.
            Bio-based and biodegradable alternatives exist but have not 
             seen widespread commercial success due to their relatively
             high cost and poor mechanical properties, this paper seeks to
             address the question of whether bio-based branching thermoset
             polyesters can be optimised using Bayesian optimisation such
             that desirable properties such as extent of reaction might be
             improved upon.
            This is achieved by identifying a bio-based and biodegradable
             polymer (poly(glycerol citrate itaconate)), identifying a
             desirable objective variable (mass loss under esterification
             as proxy for reaction extent)
             and some tunable predictor variables (monomeric stoichiometry),
             after which sequential Bayesian techniques have been used to
             sample-efficiently uncover well-performing samples.
            A Bayesian technique employing the `probability of improvement'
             acquisition function discovered the experimental global optimum
             stoichiometry producing a mass loss of 20.77~\% in curing 
             polyester samples, this constituted 72~\% of the theoretical 
             maximum, indicating a high extent of polymerisation.
            Meanwhile, an alternate sequential Bayesian technique deploying
             the `expected improvement' acquisition function displayed high
             sample efficiency, outperforming the strongest benchmark set by a
             one-shot grid sampler in 63\% of the samples.
            Both these sequential Bayesian techniques were simulated 1,000
             times by querying an emulation of the practical experiment,
             respectively exhibiting a probability of discovering the 
             experimental global optimum of 1.1\% and 5.3\%.
            Bayesian optimisation techniques represent a practically
             deployable and sample-efficient means by which desirable
             properties of bio-based thermesets may be improved.
        \end{normalsize}
    \end{justify}
\end{center}
