\begin{center}
    \begin{large}
        \textbf{Abstract}
    \end{large}
    \begin{justify}
        \begin{normalsize}
            Glass reinforced plastic composites are ubiquitous in the wind turbine manufacturing industry, and are commonly manufactured using general-purpose unsaturated polyester resin thermosets.
            Unfortunately, these resins are derived from oil-derived aromatic monomers making them both unsustainably sourced and hard to recycle.
            Bio-based and biodegradable alternatives exist but have not seen widespread commercial success; this paper seeks to address the question of whether bio-based branching thermoset polyesters can be optimised using Bayesian optimisation such that desirable properties are improved upon.
            This is achieved by identifying a bio-based and biodegradable polymer, poly(glycerol citrate itaconate), identifying a desirable objective variable and some tunable predictor variables, and finally using Bayesian optimisation to sample-efficiently identify well-performing samples.
            The most salient findings included the Bayesian optimisation routines successfully optimising the proxy variable in a sample efficient fashion, improving upon common deterministic techniques such as grid sampling.
        \end{normalsize}
    \end{justify}
\end{center}
